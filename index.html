Your goal is to build a product and charge for it. More customers, more revenue, more growth.

The takeaway is simple...

You need a development team you can trust. To build the thing that people really want to use takes intensity, experimentation, and a deep curiosity throughout the process: to figure out where and how to enter the market, to figure out how to make users genuinely better people, to reveal how to any choice that's not you is a liability.

Magic happens when you work with the right people.

Developers are the human link in the chain who're closest to the machine, and they will see things differently from their understanding of both people, and computer. The best developers will seek an acute understanding of the bigger picture, the whole context you and your idea fit into.

Choosing the right team will carry you through the frank conversations, the difficult choices, the ambiguity of creating something entirely new and give you access to the results you're really after...

More customers, more revenue, more growth.
